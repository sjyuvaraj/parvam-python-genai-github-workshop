NLP - Natural Language Processing

It is used to understand or translate the prompt or text given by user to machine.

It acts like a bridge b/w user & computer 

It can understand the text & speech from user to be converted to provide the answer.

Core Levels of NLP:
1) Tokenization:
Breaking of each words given in the prompt

Ex: "I like to learn Coding using AI"
token - 7

"I don't like to eat Bitter Guard"
token - 8 (Because don't - do + not)
lly, I'm - I + am

2) Parts-of-Speech Tagging: (POS Tagging)
Classify the nouns, pronouns, verbs & adjectives

Ex: I like to train students
"like" - verb
"train" - verb
"students" - noun

3) NER (Named Entity Relationship) **:
It is going to find the names, dates, relationship from the given prompt

Ex: Akshay is travelling to Tirupati on Saturday via Train and returning back on Sunday via Bus.

4) Sentiment Analysis:
It is going to find the emotion and tone of the sentence. (Positive, Negative & Neutral)

Ex: 
1) I liked the movie screenplay but CGI was below par.
2) I loved the product, display is amazing but battery is very poor and it gives 8 hours of SOT
3) Highly Recommended Product, Battery runs too long for 5 Hours (Sarcasm)

5) Language Modelling:
Mainly used for Tranlations and prediction of words 

Ex: Google Translate, Duolingo, Gmail recommendation, Auto-Correct Spelling

6) Semantic Parsing:
Undersands the meaning & relationship of the sentence.

Ex: Akshay is explaning about NLP to AIML batch students in CEC College for AI Workshop

Akshay - Sender
AIML Students - Reciever
NLP - Topic
Place - CEC
Program - AI Workshop
